Things to look out for while browsing news sites:
  • Political bias - does this source have a liberal or conservative bias?

  • Corporate bias - is this source biased towards certain corporations?

  • Clickbait - 'Clickbait' articles are often lacking in substance and written by people with little to no credentials.

  • Gossip - Gossip articles are usually lacking in substance and often contain misinformation.

  • Misinformation - is any information in this article contrary to common knowledge or reliable information?

  • Date news source was established- newer news sources are less likely to be trusted due to lack of experience. However, all news sources regardless of age may be prone to errors described above.

  • Author credentials- authors lacking credentials are less likely to be trusted.

  • Excessive linking to other sources - other unverified news sources may be subject to bias and misinformation as described above.
